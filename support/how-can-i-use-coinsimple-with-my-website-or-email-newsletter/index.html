How can I use CoinSimple with my Website or Email Newsletter?

This guide explains how to create a button or link on your website, or on your email newsletter, so that you can sell your products and services using CoinSimple. This approach is most useful if you have an HTML-based website or email newsletter; If your website runs on Magento, Wordpress eCommerce, or WooCommerce, please read the relevant articles for more detailed instructions.

Note: If your website runs Magento, WooCommerce or Wordpress eCommerce, please read the following support articles for Magento, WooCommerce and Wordpress eCommerce, respectively.

It is very easy to sell your products and services online through CoinSimple. Even if you don’t have a website that runs specialized eCommerce software, with CoinSimple you can create a link or a button that will allow your customers to pay you in bitcoin. As usual, your bitcoin will end up in your wallet (Electrum, Armory, Trezor or any other bitcoin address), bitcoin processor account (BitPay, Coinbase, GoCoin), or bitcoin exchange, with CoinSimple “doing all the work” and without ever touching any of your bitcoins.

In order to do that, you need to create a “Button” by going to the CoinSimple web application and clicking on “Buttons”. The process is very similar to that for creating an invoice using CoinSimple and sending it via email, see detailed instructions here) with a few differences:

  1. When you create a button you do not need to enter the name and email of you customer. That information will be filled by your customer the moment they click on your website’s button.

  2. You need to specify a webpage where the user will be sent (“redirected”) after they make a payment. Think of this page as the “Thank you, your order is being processed” page that eCommerce stores have. You should create such a page on your website for your customers. This is the “Redirect URI” that you will see on the form for creating a button on CoinSimple.

  3. If your website can receive notifications for payments, you can enter the “Callback URI” on the form for creating a button on CoinSimple. That way your website will know that a payment is made, and it could potentially make a page available to a certain user. If you have a simple website or a newsletter, and you don’t have the ability to modify your pages based on notifications, you can leave the “Callback URI” empty. Just make sure that you check your email or CoinSimple dashboard to find out if a customer has paid, so that you can ship your product.

With CoinSimple, websites of any complexity can be easily set up to accept bitcoin. Please use this feature and let us know what you think on Twitter or at support@coinsimple.com.
